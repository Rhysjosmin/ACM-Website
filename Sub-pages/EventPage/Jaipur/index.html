DBCE ACM Student Chapter Visits Jaipur


The DBCE ACM Chapter, the Computer Engineering department's ACM chapter at Don Bosco College of Engineering, recently took a tour to Jaipur, the "Pink City" of India. The purpose of this trip was to provide the members with a practical learning experience about the production pipeline and manufacturing process in various industries. The chapter visited Hindware, Purnima University, and a marble processing unit during their trip. At Hindware, the members learned about the production pipeline of sanitary ware products. They were introduced to new concepts and technologies that are being developed by the students of the university. The members also got an opportunity to interact with the experts in the field and learn from their experience. At Purnima University, the DBCE ACM members got an insight into the education system in India. The students of Purnima University showcased their innovative ideas, and the ACM members learnt about the new concepts being developed in the fields of technology and engineering. The members were impressed with the level of creativity and innovation demonstrated by the students. The visit to the marble processing unit was a unique experience for the members. They got an opportunity to learn about the manufacturing process of marble, right from the extraction of raw material to the finished product. The members witnessed the process of marble cutting, polishing, and shaping, and gained an understanding of the skill and precision required to produce high-quality marble products. After exploring the industrial side of Jaipur, the members took some time to explore the beauty of the city. They visited the marble dump, which was a dumpsite for marble pieces. Although it was a dump, the beauty of the marble pieces was mesmerizing, and the members felt like they had entered a place from a fairytale. Over the next few days, the DBCE ACM members visited the forts and learnt about their history. They explored the intricate architecture and the unique design elements of each fort. The members were fascinated by the rich history and culture of Jaipur. Apart from the educational experiences, the members had a lot of fun during their trip. They indulged in the local cuisine and shopped for souvenirs. The trip provided the ACM members with an opportunity to bond and learn together, and they returned with a newfound appreciation for the industrial and cultural diversity of Jaipur. The DBCE ACM Chapter aims to provide practical learning experiences to its members, and this tour was a step in that direction. The members got to witness the production process firsthand and learn from experts in the field. The trip also gave them an opportunity to explore the beauty and culture of Jaipur. In conclusion, the tour to Jaipur was a memorable experience for the DBCE ACM members. It not only gave them a chance to learn about the production pipeline and manufacturing process in different industries but also allowed them to explore the beauty and culture of Jaipur. The DBCE ACM chapter hopes to organize more such trips in the future, providing practical learning experiences to its members.
